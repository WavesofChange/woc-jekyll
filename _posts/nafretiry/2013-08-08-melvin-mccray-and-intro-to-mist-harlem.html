Harlem throught the eyes of a diffrent set of youth!

We went to Mist and met another set of students and it was pretty interesting. Melvin McCray is a very talented, and interesting character. He told us stories of people who visited MIST Harlem and how tickets were sold out for shows there. Also, Melvin told us he was an editor for ABC since 1981. What Melvin does is interesting; he does photography and videography with his students. He showed us some interesting videos of interviews and how he used pictures and things to create a visual of what people are trying to show us through their eyes. Also, Melvin tried to show us harlem back then from anothers eyes, and harlem now through our eyes. Melvin and his students found pictures of buildings back then, and took pictures of what they look like now. We took pictures which was unexpected. It was interesting because of the rotation process and the roles each person had; for example: if one person was taking my picture, another was taking a picture of them, and others would help adjust lighting, some would observe, then they would rotate. From my eyes it did not seem easy at all, and his students are lucky to be working with him. My experience at Mist was inspiring.
